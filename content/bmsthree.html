CHAPTER 03 : ENERGY MANAGEMENT

Introduction:

Management is the technique and process of planning, organizing, arranging materials, financial, human and other require resources for sel ecting, supervising, monitoring to achieve the objectives. Energy management is important to reduce the consumption and valuable resources.

Meaning of Energy Management:

Energy management is the process of monitoring, controlling and conserving energy in the organization.

Significance of Energy Management:

The increasing population, transport and standard of leaving, the demand for energy is increasing while available resources for energy are less. Now a days energy is considered as important source for industry, transportation, agriculture, commercial and domestic purpose.

Importance of Energy Management:

Energy should be considered as business cost, like raw materials or labour. Companies can achieve reduction in energy bills by implementing simple house keeping method. By this method they can achieve,

  • Reduces Cost: most of the organization can save up to 20% by managing the energy use.
  • Reduces Carbon Emissions: If reducing energy consumption is also reduces the carbon emission and environmental effects.
  • Develop Green Image: By reducing carbon emission it will helps to build a green image and generating good business opportunities.
  • Reduce Risk: Reducing energy helps to reduce risk of energy cost fluctuations.
  • Utilizing Energy Resources: Energy management involves utilizing the available energy resources more effectively that is minimum cost.

Energy Conservation:

Energy conservation means to reduce the quantity of energy that is used for different purposes. This practice may results in increase of financial capital, environmental value, national and personal security and human comfort.

Need of Energy Conservation:

The followings are the needs of energy conservation.

  • Energy conservation is a single step which can effectively contribute towards reduction of storages.
  • If everyone started saving energy then cost of leaving can be save.

Objectives of Energy Management:

  • To minimize energy costs.

It is a term used to refer handling technical data related building it includes gathering information that is required for minimizing energy use.

  • To focus on process and resource for reducing the energy speed.

  • To support decision such as system are process efficiency, rating and critical area.

Methodology of EFMS:

The various methodology of EFMS are

  • Visualization of module.
  • Alarming module.
  • Work order module.
  • Data source module.
  • Reporting module.

Visualization Module:It will present a graphical representation energy information alarming module.

Alarming Module:It will create and management notification of alarm base engine values for any fault detection.

Work order Module:It will manage action of operator related to reach faults cause, detection, diagnosis, action and evaluation.

Data source Module:It will manage store the various data source from the energy management system.

Reporting Module:It will range the creation & distribution of energy & facility report.

Various Types of Information:

The various types of information class are -

  • Energy Conservation: Information or energy consumption information it can be commonly handled by EMS from online metering devices such as energy meter and gas meter.
  • System Information: Such as (HVAC) heating ventilation & air conditioning, sensor readings etc.
  • Building System Information: Such as building size, floor number & area, cooling capacity of HVAC and maintenance log etc.
  • Weather Information: Commonly derived from inter weather feedback local installed sensor.
  • Utility Builds: Information commonly related to energy accounting.

Process of EFMS:

Following are different types of process perform by EFMS.

  • Work Order Process: It include process for alert to user managing the action they under take until problem is resolved.
  • Reporting Process: It includes creation of reports of data mining & fault investigations.
