CHAPTER 01 : BASIC MANAGEMENT SKILLS

Introduction:

The basic management skills consist of a inter personal skills that a person uses to interact with other people. It is also called as people skill or communication skill. It consists of 2 types of skills.

  1. Hard Skills
  2. Soft Skills

Hard skills is nothing but which appear on resume such as education, working experience and level of expertise.

Soft skills are a term which refers to personality development of a person, type of language, personal habits, friendliness and other.

Importance of Knowing Yourself:

Understanding and knowing yourself is very important in our life, this will help us to develop good relations with everyone.

  • It helps to control emotions.
  • It helps to take better decisions.
  • It helps to improve relationships.
  • It helps to improve happiness and joy.
  • It helps to reach your goals.
  • It helps to lead our life in a better way.
  • It helps to improve our personality.
  • It helps to change our attitude and improve our values.
  • It helps to become more competent and acceptable by others.
  • Helps to realize and improve your full potential.

Process of Knowing Yourself:

Maintain a personal diary: Find 5 to 10 minutes every day and choose a comfortable place to write. Write about how you feel, your likes and dislikes, what trouble you and how to establish a connection.

Practice meditation: Find 10 minutes every day and sit in a comfort place. Focus on breath and allow good thoughts to enter in your mind.

Exercise regularly: Regular exercise helps a person physically and mentally strong.

Go for a walk regularly: If exercise is not possible than go for a walking because walking is a moving meditation.

Do some driving: Driving proves to be a good process particularly when you find a hard times. Do not forget to take your electronic items to listen your favorite music.

Do some outings: Practice a habit of going out for sight-seeing particularly when down with something in life. A day spent in the park, a theater, coffee house, etc., helps you lot.

Do some hobbies: Identify the hobbies of your early days. Try to do things that you used to do those days. It will give you a chance to identify your passion within you. Hobbies helps you to find some time to relax.

SWOT Analysis:

SWOT stands for Strength, Weakness, Opportunities and Threats. It is a useful tool for understanding and decision making in all situation of personal and business organization. The main aim of this analysis is to identify the internal and external factors that are important to achieve the objective. It is a general tool designed for a decision making purpose.

Benefits of SWOT Analysis:

  • It is simple to the participants.
  • They can grasp the concepts and process easily.
  • It can be practiced at individual and corporate level.
  • Less expansive – SWOT analysis can be done internally provided the internal facilitator has the experience to manage.
  • Inclusive – It allows the participation of team and results are more likely to represent the real environments.
  • Understand your business better.
  • Address weaknesses.
  • Deter threats.
  • Capitalize on opportunities.
  • Take advantage of your strengths.
  • Develop business goals and strategies for achieving them.

Uses of SWOT Analysis:

  • Strategic planning, brainstorming and decision making.
  • Building on strengths.
  • Minimizing weaknesses.
  • Seizing opportunities.
  • Counteracting threats.
  • Addressing individual issues.

Perception:

Perception refers to the way in which a person experiences the world. Perception is a process by which people organize, interpret and experience the ideas, or interact with the people.

Perception is “a process by which individuals organize and interpret their sensory impression in order to give meaning to their environment”. The process of perception helps us to manage noises, smell, slight, taste received from the environment and gives a meaning to them. It places an important role in an organization particularly in the field of recruitment, selecting, positioning and promotions. During this process the people need to understand each other properly.

The following activities are helps to improve perception:

  • Perceiving or understanding others better.
  • Have positive attitude.
  • Avoid distortion.
  • Open communication.

In simple words, the perception is a process which involves seeing, receiving, selecting, organizing, interpreting and giving meaning to the environment.

Improving Perception:

  • Perceiving oneself accurately: One need to improve more awareness about himself / herself.
  • Improving one’s self-concept: When people successfully accomplish what they want, it develops a sense of self-regard and self-esteem. It is called self-concept.
  • Be empathetic: Empathy means to be able to see a situation as it is perceived by other people. In a way, it is like putting your feel in another’s shoes.
  • Having positive attitude: Positive attitude makes one’s perception positive or more accurate.
  • Avoiding perceptual distortion: Some factors such as hallo effect, stereotyping, attribution, first impression etc. distort one’s perception about things or problems.
  • Communicating openly: Experience suggest that sometimes perception get distorted due to communication gap or and inadequate communication.

Communication:

Communication means process of sharing our ideas, thoughts and feelings with other peoples. When we communicate we speak, listen and observe. It is a process of exchange of ideas between two or more peoples. It is a process of passing and understanding information from one person to another person.

Special Features of Communication:

The special features of communication are

  • Communication is a two-way traffic.
  • Communication is unavoidable.
  • Communication is a social process.
  • Communication is a continuous process.
  • Communication is universal.

Communication Process Model:

Sender: The communication process begins with sender. Sender sends a message to the receiver.

Receiver: Receiver is the person for whom the communication effort is take place.

Encoding: It is a process of transferring the information into a correct form by which the receiver can understand.

Decoding: It is the process by which the receiver translates the information into meaningful information.

Transmission channel: A channel is the medium of transmission of information from one person to another.

Feedback: It is the reaction of the receiver when he receives the information from the sender. The communication process is completed through feedback.

Channel of communication:

Some channels of communication which is best and most economical for you and your organization for communication are listed.

  • Use of telephone
  • Use of radio
  • Use of television
  • Use of news letter
  • Use of invitation
  • Use of post card
  • Use of personal card
  • Use of e-mails
  • Use of Fax

Formal and Informal Communication Network:

Formal Communication Network:

This network is designed by the management to dictate who should talk to whom to get job done. It is created to regulate the flow of communication as to avoid any confusion and make it more orderly, timely and smooth.

Informal Communication Network:

It is not intentionally formed network by the management. It arises to meet the needs spontaneously that are not satisfied by formal process. In fact this is the network formed by the workers without the knowledge of the management in canteen, playground, cinema hall where the workers meet informally and build the informal relationship by communicating with each other without considering their branch, post, position, etc. It is also called as grapevine communication.

People communicate through symbols, signs, behavior, signals, speech, words, listening, silence, eye contact, head movement, postures, mannerism etc.

Barriers of Communication:

  • Unknown language
  • Unknown words
  • Unknown symbols
  • Unknown sign
  • Noise
  • Improper time
  • Distance
  • Information overloaded
  • Emotions

Tips for Effective Communication:

  • Know yourself: A good communicator knows what it is talking with the customer. Knowing yourself is necessary for effective communication.
  • Be focus: A person should be clear and focus on the topic to be communicated. It should be short, straight and simple sentence.
  • Audiences: You must know the kind of audience you are addressing. If the audience is children’s or adults you may have to adapt a simple communication. If scientist or social worker than communication must be technically strong.
  • Be positive: The person who is going to communicate must believe in himself and communicate with the receiver using good words and known language.
  • Location: The person who is going to communicate must visit the location once.

Team Building:

A team is a small group of people with skills and abilities to reach a common goal.

Team is a group of people working toward common objective.

Team building is one of the difficult area in which the employees are to be trained. Effective teams are necessary for growth, development and management of organization. The success of an organization depends upon the kind of team. So team work has become the important concept in organization.

Aspects of team building: The team has to be identified by giving the unique name for the team organizes special tips and training for the team. Have special logo and a kit.

Characteristics of Effective Team:

There are several characteristics of an effective team. These include –

  • The team is having clear purpose or mission or vision.
  • The team is having adequate resources.
  • It has a proper leadership and structure of positions.
  • The team has a climate of informal, comfortable and relaxed.
  • There is a participative environment.
  • Climate of trust.
  • Performance evaluation and reward system.
  • Members are open minded, committed to goals, listen to others.
  • Consensus decision.
  • Shared leadership.
  • Open communication.
  • It mobilizes the external relations.
  • Self assignment – checks the performance itself.
  • Member’s flexibility and preferences.
  • Stimulating activities.
  • Enjoyable – members enjoy being the part of team with sense of belongingness and social involvement.
  • A team has a greater efficiency with proper motivation.
  • Civilized – each member should be co-operative with low level of conflict.

Role of Team Leader:

  • Provide team leadership and coaching
  • Focus on team task
  • Co-ordinate team logistic
  • Communicate
  • Personal role

Role of Team Members:

The members of the team should play the following roles:

  • Care about each other – to have effective team efforts.
  • Open and truthful – to step forward and to get good result.
  • Maintain the high levels of trust.
  • Consensus decisions.
  • Committed to have excellence for getting results they desired.
  • Maintaining the low level of conflict.
  • Real listening – to have effective communication.
  • Express feelings – to keep emotions and feelings out of the workplace.

Skills Needed for Teamwork:

  • Listening: It is important to listen to others people’s ideas.
  • Questioning: It is important to ask questions, interact and discus the objectives of the team.
  • Changing: It is important to exchange, rethink and if needed change the ideas.
  • Respecting: It is important to respect and accept the ideas of others.
  • Helping: It is important principle of team work.
  • Sharing: It creates good environment for the team work.
  • Participating: It makes the work simple and easy.
  • Communication: To work effectively in a team you have to do a good communication within the team.

Model of Team Building:

  • Forming: This is a process of getting members with one other and making a part of a group or team.
  • Storming: This is a process of asking question between the members of the group but at the end of the members come together toward common goal or task.
  • Norming: This is a process of developing close relationship within the members.
  • Performing: This is a stage of developing good performance in the team work.
  • Adjourning: This is a stage of separation. The members of the group are separated for two reasons.
    1. The group might have completed its task.

      2. OR

    2. The members decide to close the group.
