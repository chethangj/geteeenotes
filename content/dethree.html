CHAPTER 03 : BOOLEAN ALGEBRA AND LOGIC GATES

If an expression has all variables and a value then the value is called constant.

1 = Constant

Compliment:

It is represented by a bar over the letter (variable).

Example:

\[Y = \bar A\]

\[A = 0,\;\bar A = 1\] \[A = 1,\bar A = 0\]

Boolean function (expression):

The expression constructed by connecting the Boolean constant and variable with Boolean operation is known as Boolean function.

Literal:

Each occurrence of variable in Boolean function either in complemented or un-complemented form is called literal.

\(A = 1\) , \(B = 1\) , \(\bar A = 0\) , \(\overline B = 0\)

Laws of Boolean algebra:

  1. Commutative law
  2. Associated law
  3. Distribution law

1. Commutative Law:

1) A+B = B+A

2) A.B = B.A

2. Associated Law:

1) (A+B)+C = A+(B+C)

3. Distribution Law:

A(B+C)=AB+AC

Identities of Boolean addition:

  1. \(A + 0 = A\)
  2. \(A + 1 = A\left( 1 \right)\)
  3. \(A + A = A\left( 1 \right)\)
  4. \(A + \bar A = 1\)
  5. \(0 + 1 = 1\)

Identities of Boolean multiplication:

  1. \(A.0 = 0\)
  2. \(A.1 = A\left( 1 \right)\)
  3. \(A.\bar A = 0\)
  4. \(A.A = A\left( 1 \right)\)

DeMorgan’s Theorem:

\[\overline {AB} = \bar A + \bar B\] \[\overline {A + B} = \bar A.\bar B\]

Statement 1: The compliment of a product is equal to sum of the compliments.

\[\overline {AB} = \bar A + \bar B\]

Statement 2: The compliment of a sum is equal to the product of the compliments.

\[\overline {A + B} = \bar A.\bar B\]

Logic Gates:

  • Logic gate is a circuit with one or more inputs but only one output.
  • These are the fundamental building blocks of digital system.
  • NOT, AND, OR, gates are the basic gates.
  • The interconnections of gates perform verity of logical operation hence it is called logic design.

SOP (Sum of Product):

  • Product of two or more variables in boolean algebra is AND function and sum is the OR function.
  • Sum of product expression is two or more AND function ORed together.
  • The individual term in SOP is called min term.

POS (Product of Sum):

  • Product of sum expression is two or more OR functions ANDed together.
  • The individual term in POS is called max term.

Karnaugh Map (K-Map):

It is a graphical tool used to simplify logic equations. A truth table can be converted to its logic circuit in a simple and orderly using K-Map.

Rules for K-Map simplification:

  • Plot the K-Map and places 1’s in those all corresponding to the 1’s in the truth table or SOP expression. Place 0’s in the other cells.
  • Check the K-Map for adjacent 1’s and encircles those 1’s which are not adjacent to any other 1’s. These are called isolated 1’s.
  • Check for those 1’s which are an adjacent to only one other 1’s and encircles such pairs.
  • Check for pairs, quads and octets of adjacent is even if it contains some 1’s that have already been encircle. While doing this make show that these are minimum number of groups.
  • Combine any pairs necessary to include any 1’s that have not yet been proved.
  • From the simplified expression by summing product terms of all the groups.
