CHAPTER 06 : SAFETY AND ENVIRONMENTAL ISSUES

Introduction on Industrial Safety:

Industrial safety is becoming a very important aspect of heavy losses to both in employees and employers due to accidents. Lacks of workers loss their life every year in the world due to industrial accidents. The industrial safety announcement was started in 1912 to the govt. of India to provide a safety to the industry and also the industrial workers.

Need for Industrial Safety Measure:

The industrial safety is very important because every year more number of industrial accidents are occur result in temporary or permanent disable or death of employees. It also involves heavy losses from waste in man hours, machine hours, compensation amount, training for new employees etc.

Safety measures result in improving the conditions under which workers are employed it improves not only their physical efficiency but also provide protection to their life and parts of there is no safety measures in factories. It may lead to increase in the number of accidents, human failure due to carelessness, ignorance, inadequate skill and improper super vision as also contributed to accidents.

Other factors increase need for safety measures are.

  • Complex industrialization in manufacturing process.
  • Expansion or modification in existing factories.
  • Setting up new industries involving hazards not more earlier.
  • Financial requirements due to accidents.

Role of Safety in Industry:

In order to avoid accidents govt. as created safety acts, as per these acts the industries have to provide appropriate safety measures in the industry, employees and to the workplace, the safety in industry helps in,

  • Increasing the production rate.
  • Reducing the production cost.
  • Reducing the damage to the machineries and equipmentâ€™s.
  • Preventing accidents.
  • Increases the profit of the company.

Accidents:

Accidents can be defined as sudden change in action or the un-expectable event which results in temporary or permanent disablement of employees and involves heavy losses.

Accidents can also be defined as occurrence that interfere the progress of the activity. It can be prevented by taking the safety measures in the industry.

Losses due to Accidents:

It can be classified into 2 types.

  1. Direct Loss
  2. Indirect Loss

1. Direct Loss:

  • Compensation insurance to be paid for the employee.
  • Payment and overhead cost has to be pay.
  • Cost of medical care and hospital.

2. Indirect Loss:

  • Damage to the equipment, material and plant.
  • Loss of time of the other workers.
  • Loss of time of safety engineers, supervisors and staff to involved in the investigating, recording and other activities related to that accidents.
  • Loss due to production delay by the accidents.
  • Replacing the injured employee.
  • Reduction in efficiency of the injured worker.

Causes of Accidents:

1. Technical Causes: It is further classified as

i. Electrical and Mechanical Factors.

Technical causes are due to inefficiencies in plant, equipment, tools, material handling system.

The electrical and mechanical factors are,

  • Unsafe mechanical design.
  • Poor electrical fittings.
  • Unsafe work place.
  • Defective devices.
  • Improper material handling system.
  • Leakage of gas or oil.
  • Improper maintenance of machines.

ii. Environmental Factors.

  • Too low temperature in workplace.
  • Too high temperature in workplace.
  • High speed of work due to heavy load.
  • Noise, bad smell and poor housekeeping.
  • Restless work.
  • Improper ventilation.

2. Human Causes.

  • Age
  • Health
  • Home environment
  • Financial position
  • Lack of knowledge and skills.
  • Improper attitude towards work.
  • Mental worries.
  • Improper use of machines and tools.
  • Delay in implementation of safety measures.
  • No interest about the safety by the management.

Prevention of Accident:

Accident prevention is very essential in a industry in order to reduce the operating and production cost and to have the good employee and employer relation.

Hence the following preventive measures have to be implemented.

  1. Safe workplace and working conditions.
  2. Safe material handling equipment.
  3. Personal protective devices.

i. Safe workplace and working conditions:

  • Every employ should have space to move and operate.
  • Good ventilation should be provided at work place.
  • Sufficient lighting and shock proof working place should be provided.
  • There should not be any fire hazard.
  • Air, temperature, purity should be maintained.
  • Noise proof working condition should be there.

ii. Safe material handling equipment:

  • Properly maintain lifting machines, chains, and ropes should be used.
  • Every lift shall be good construction and good strength.
  • Every lifts, cranes and hoist shall be sufficiently protected.

iii. Personal protective devices:

  • For protection of head safety hard hats should be used.
  • For protection of face safety mask should be used.
  • For protection of eyes goggles or spectacle should be used.
  • For protection of other body parts, apron, hand gloves, safety shoes is to be used.

Safety Committee:

The primary purpose of safety committee is to promote safety awareness and reduce the injuries. The safety committee is headed by safety director and includes representatives from each department. The total number of committee members should not Exide 5. Individual member can be volunteers or appointed.

All meetings are to be take place in conference room. Each meeting should have a set of topics and each meeting should be recorded. A copy of meeting shall be forwarded to executive wise president within 48 hours of meeting.

Each safety committee meeting shall include a review of past month as well as next month.

Role of Safety Committee:

The safety committee is form for promoting safety in the industry. This committee consists of members from both management and workers. The representatives of the committee are managers, safety engineer, doctor, supervisor and union leaders. All these representatives collectively explained safety practices to the workers.

The safety committee will visit the factory and give instruction periodically about the safety. It also gives training about safety. The committee will display safety banners which contains percussions, rules to be followed by each employee at the workplace. It conducts safety exhibitions and the awards will be given to the worker who works safety without meeting an accident. At the end of the each month the committee will make arrangements for screening video films about safety at workplace.

General Functions of Safety Committee:

  • Identify the workplace hazard.
  • Enforcement of safety rules.
  • Measuring the safety performance.
  • Reducing the frequency of injuries.
  • Creating the safety policies.
  • Developing and monitoring the safety programs.

Role of Safety Engineer in an Industry:

  • He should provide necessary training to the workers about safety methods.
  • He should advise supervisor staff regarding safety practices.
  • He should maintain accidents reports submitted by supervisor and details of injury.
  • He should inform the management about unsafe practices.
  • He should estimate amount of danger involves due to accidents.
  • He should display safety banners and postures at different places.

Role of Foreman in Implementing Safety Practices in Industry:

  • He must inspect the machines from time to time.
  • He must watch the workers weather they are using protective devices when they are at work.
  • He should be capable of providing first aid treatment to the injured system.
  • He should make proper arrangement to shift injured person to the nearest hospital.
  • He must prepare an accident report at the spot.
  • He should advise supervisory staff regarding safety practices.
  • He should maintain accidents report submitted by supervisor.
  • He should inform to the management about unsafe practices in the industry.

What are the Safety Measures Prevent accidents in an Industry?

  • By providing proper fencing all machines.
  • The lifting device such as lift, cranes, pulley etc. should not be loaded above the limits.
  • Women or young person and unskilled worker will not be allowed to clean, lubricate or adjust a machine which is running. Only skilled operator should be permitted to handle such operations.
  • Proper layout, ventilation and proper illumination should be provided.
  • Provision for firefighting equipment, proper warning signals and storing of explosives should be provided.
  • Make provision for emergency exit and first aid arrangement.
  • No young persons should be allowed to work on dangerous machines.
  • Conducting safety awareness program in industry and display safety charts in the industries.
  • Training should be given to the workers about safety measures.
  • Every lift, cranes, elevators, hoist must be of good mechanical strength and it should be properly examine at least once in six months.
  • All the workers in the workplace must wear personal protective devices.
  • Operating instructions and procedure should be fixed near each machine.
  • All floors, staircase, passages and emergency exits should be free from oil, dust and grease which cause person to slip.
  • Every vessel, sump, tank or any opening in the ground must be covered with proper cover with fencing.
  • It is prohibited in a factory to employ a person to lift a heavy load which cause him injuries.
  • The workplace must be free from dust, gas, fume or vapor.
  • Provision has been made to escape in the case of fire.

Ozone Layer and its Depletion:

A layer of ozone about 20 to 25 km is present in the atmosphere in very small quantities. It filters the harmful ultraviolet radiations and prevents them from reaching the surface of the earth. Thus this ozone layer as a shield protecting the life on earth from the harmful effects of UV radiations found sun radiations.

The stratosphere contains a layer of ozone that protects surface from UV radiations coming from the sun. However some pollution enters the stratosphere there by depleting it. The ozone reacts with florin, water vapors and nitrogen oxide released by high air craft, chloro fluro carbon (CFC) which are widely used in refrigerators and fire extinguishers, huge gases, smoke and chemicals emitted by industrial plants and automobiles.

Volcanic eruption is also depleting ozone because they emit sulphur in the atmosphere.

The rate of production of CFC continues at the same rate then the rate of ozone decomposition would increase by accumulation of chlorine in the stratosphere which further depletes the ozone layer.

Process of Ozone Layer Depletion:

The chloro fluro carbon (CFC) used in refrigerators and air conditioners slowly rise in atmosphere. It contains chlorine, fluorine and carbon. When they reach in to the stratosphere they broke down by reaction with UV rays from the sun. This causes them to release chlorine. The chlorine reacts with ozone and destroys ozone molecules hence depletion of ozone layer occurs.

Effects of Ozone Depletion:

Human health is adversely affected and it also affects the agricultural process. Following are some of the effects of ozone depletion.

  • Earth excuse exposure to UV radiations leads to several health problems in humans such as eye problems, skin cancer.
  • It damages the immune system (reduced resistance to decrease).
  • Shorter life of plastic and paint.
  • Restricted growth and crop damage.
  • Destruction of water life.

Global Warming:

The increase of carbon dioxide (CO2) in the atmosphere leads to greenhouse effect which further causes gradual heating of earth called global warming.

The primary source of carbon dioxide is the consumption of fossil fuels. The cement manufacturing will also contribute to increase CO2 in the atmosphere.

The major absorption of CO2 are plants and oceans, but production of CO2 is faster than its absorption. Therefore the level of CO2 is increasing in the atmosphere. This increase of CO2 in atmosphere which influences the climate is called the greenhouse effect. The layer of CO2 acts like a glass in a greenhouse it allows the sun rays to pass through it and heat up the earth but it does not allow the heat radiations re-emit by earth. The net result of all these is increase in heating of earth which is called global warming.

Effects of Global Warming:

  • It increases the earth temperature by 40 to 60.
  • If earth continuous to warm then all the glaciers (ice caps in Antarctic) and the arctic will begin to melt. So that the sea level increases and most of the cities on the sea shore will be sub merged.
  • The global warming reduces the natural phenomenon of the nature.
  • The global warming disturbs the normal life of the earth.
  • It affects the climatic changes.

Remedies against Global Warming:

  • Reduction of deforestation and increase in forest.
  • The restriction on automobiles and industries.
  • Development of community base factories.
  • Strict low against the usage of fuel.

Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA):

It is a process of evaluating the environmental impact of a project taking into account which relate social economic, cultural and human health impact, both beneficial and adverse effect.

It can be also defined as a tool use to identify environmental, social and economic impacts of a project to take decisions.

Aim of EIA:

  • Modify and improve design.
  • Protect human health and safety.
  • Avoid serious and inversible damage to the environment.
  • Identify impacts and inform decision making.
  • Efficient resources use to reduce social and economic aspects.

Need of EIA:

  • It reduces environmental impact and other adverse effect of a project.
  • Waste minimizing, reuse, recycling, disposal and tracking.
  • It gives the option for decision making.
  • Cultural heritage and historic places safety.
  • Environmental factors are considered in the decision-making process.
  • Informing the public about the project.
  • To describe the project and associated works together with the environments for carrying out the developments of the projects.
  • To identify community and environment likely to be affected by the project development.
  • To design and specify environmental monitoring and audit requirements if necessary to implement and effectiveness of the environmental protection and pollution control measures.
  • To investigate the side effects of project and make the alternate arrangements for the effects.

Methodology of EIA:

1. Project Screening: In this application the EIA screening is done about project operating in a country. It may have significant environmental impacts.

2. Scoping: It identifies environmental issues to the early stage.

3. Consideration of Alternatives: It includes alternative approaches which includes project location, layout processes, process and other operating conditions.

4. Description of the Project: In this step it understands various characteristics stages of development locations and processes.

5. Description of environmental Line: It includes the establishment of both present and feature state of environment, in the absence of project. It also considered the changes resulting from natural events and from human activities.

6. Identification Key Impact: In this step it checks the adverse and beneficial process of the project on the environment.

7. The Prediction of Impact: This step is identifies the changes in the environmental the project is implemented.

8. Mitigation: It involves measures to avoid, reduce or compensate for any significant adverse impact.

9. Public Consultation and Participation: In this step the public views are taken into consideration in the decision-making process.

10. EIA Presentation: It is the important step in the process if done badly then much of good work in the EIA may be neglected.

11. Review: This involves systematic appraisal of a quality of the EIA.

12. Decision Making: At this stage decisions are made by the EIA with material consideration weather to accept or to reject the project.

13. Auditing: This step involves actual outcome with predicted outcome and it can be used to check the quality of the project.
