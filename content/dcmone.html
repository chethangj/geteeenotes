CHAPTER 01 : DC Generators Construction and Working

DC generators:

DC generator is a machine that converters mechanical energy into electrical energy.

Working principle:

DC generator works on the principle of faradays law of electromagnetic induction that is whenever a conductor cuts the magnetic lines of flux an emf is induced in that conductor.

Therefore a DC generator works on the principle of production of dynamically induced emf, the direction of induced emf is determined by Fleming’s right hand rule (generator rule).

Fleming’s right hand rule:

This rule is used to find out the direction of induced emf in the conductor. This rule states that,

If we extended first three fingers of right hand such that they are mutually parallel to each other. Then the fore figure represents the direction of flux, the thumb figure indicates the direction of motion of the conductor and the middle figure indicates the direction of induced emf.

Construction and working of DC machines / generators:

The important parts of DC generator / machines are,

  1. Yoke
  2. Eyebolt
  3. Poles
  4. Field winding
  5. Armature
  6. Commutators
  7. Brushes
  8. Bearings
  9. Base

Yoke:

It is the outer most cover of DC machine. The insulating material get protected from harmful atmospheric elements like moisture, dust etc. it provides the mechanical support to the poles. It provides the path of low reactance for magnetic flux.

Choice of material:

It is made up of cast iron because it is cheapest for log machines. Rolled steel cast iron silicon steel is used which provides high permeability and gives good mechanical strength.

Eye bolt:

It is necessary to lift the machine.

Poles:

The poles are divided into two parts. That is,

  1. Pole core
  2. Pole shoes

The pole cores are carries a field winding which is necessary to produce flux and it is direct the flux produce through air gap to armature core to the next poles.

A Pole shoe enlarges the area of armature to come across the flux and it produces large induced emf. It gives particular shape.

Choice of material:

It is made up of magnetic material like cast iron and cast steel. It has to carry flux, providing a path of low reluctance.

Field winding:

Field winding is used to carry current due to pole core. It works on the principle of electromagnetic induction to produce the flux in a magnetic field that is exiting the poles as an electromagnet. It is called field winding or exiting winding.

Choice of material:

It carries the current so it is made up of conducting material like aluminium, copper. The field windings are south pole as well as north pole.

Armature:

The armature is divided into two parts.

  1. Armature core
  2. Armature winding

a. Armature core:

Armature core is a cylindrical shape mounted to the shaft and air ducks to prevent the air flow through armature for the purpose of cooling.

The armature core provides house for armature winding that is armature conductors. It provides low reluctance of magnetic flux produce by the filed winding.

Choice of material:

The armature winding is nothing but the inter connection of the armature conductors placed in the slots provided on the armature core.

b. Armature winding

When the armature is rotated in case of generator magnetic flux get cuts by armature conductors and emf get induced in the armature winding. Generation of emf in the generator takes place in the armature winding when current supplying in case of DC motor to do the useful work in the external circuit.

Choice of material:

It is made up of copper. The conductors are placed in the armature slots which are lined with insulating material.

Commutator:

In the commutator it converters AC to DC. It is a cylindrical in shape and consists of a number of copper segments; each segment is insulated from each other by mica insulation. The emf induced in the armature conductors is alternating. It produces the unidirectional torque.

Choice of material:

The material used is high conductivity copper. It is made up of shaped segment magnet.

Brushes and brush gears:

Brushes are used for collecting the output current from the commutator and make it available to the stationary external circuit. The brushes are made up of soft material carbon. It is in rectangular shape.

The brushes are made to press on the commutator surface by means of a spring the tension can be adjusted with the help of liver. It is also avoid the wear tear commutator.

Bearings:

Bearings are used to obtain free and smooth rotation. It is fitted inside the end covers and between them armature rotates. For smaller machines ball bearings and for large machines roller bearings are used.

Properties of material used for DC machines:

  • The resistive materials used for the winding is low. The resistance afford is low so that the voltage drop across it as well as copper loses takes place are kept minimum.
  • The resistance temperature coefficient is low so that the change in temperature has small effect on the resistance values.
  • The material have high magnetic permeability, weak current can be produced by large flux.
  • The magnetic materials have high saturation limits.
  • Low reluctance so as to provide low opposition of flow of flux.
  • The resistivity of the material used for the yoke and core is high so that the eddy current losses are kept minimum.

Properties of slots insulation materials:

The important properties of slots insulation materials are,

  • Is has very high insulation resistance.
  • High dielectric strength.
  • Low dielectric losses.
  • High melting point so as to withstand high temperature.
  • It has the capable of withstanding repeated heat cycle.

Slots insulation materials:

The various slots insulation materials used in DC machines is,

  • A very common slot insulation material used is called DMD (Dacron Mylar and Dacron) for lamination.
  • Polyester film with insulation fish papers are used because it has a high mechanical strength.
  • Two layers of polyester non ovens like cotton paper and electric grade polyester film laminated.
  • Two layers of polyester fiber paper with one layer of polyester film. It is suitable for small and medium size machines.
  • Mica split rings based composite craft paper, glass abettors cloth bonding resins are also used for the slot insulation in case of two or four pole machines.
  • Cardboard is mainly uses as slot liners.

Types of armature winding:

There are numbers armature conductors which are connected in specific manner as per the requirement is called armature winding.

Basically the armature winding is divided into two types.

  1. Lap winding
  2. Wave winding

1. Lap winding:

In the lap winding the conductor connection is in slot one then the connections are overlap each other as shown in the figure.

The over lapping of the winding is as shown in the figure. The total numbers of conductor are getting divided into poles (P) that is no. of parallel path. Where P= no. of poles in the machine.



2. Wave winding:

This type of winding avoids the overlap. It is a progressive wave that is called as wave winding. Both coils starting from a lot one and slot two are in progressive waveform.

The total no. of conductors gets into two parallel path always irrespective no. of poles (P) of the machine. The no. of parallel path in which armature conductors are divided due to lap or wave winding of connection that is denoted as A.

A=P for lap winding.
A=2 for wave winding.

Winding technology:

1) Conductor:

Conductor is a length of wire which is laying in a magnetic field where the emf is induced.

2) Turns:

Two conductors are placed in different slots when connected together forms a turns.

In the armature winding the no. of turns may specified from the no. of conductors are decided that is,

\[Z = 2 \times No.\;of\;turns\]

3) Coil:

The group of number of turns is called coil.

If the coil contains only one turn is called single turn but it has two conductors. If the coil is more than one turn is called multi turn coil. Z is the conductor used to calculate generator emf. Each coil side placed in different slots.

4) Coil span:

It is the distance between the two sides of the coil and it is expressed in terms of armature slots.

5) Pole pitch:

It is the distance between two adjacent poles the armature is divided by the number of poles.

\[Pole\;Pitch = \frac{{no.\;of\;armature\;slots/conductor}}{{no.\;of\;poles}}\]
\[Pole\;Pitch = \frac{Z}{P}\]

6) Back pitch:

It is the distance between the two sides of the coil on the back of the armature is called back pitch. It is away from the commutator. It is represented as YB.

7) Front pitch:

It is the distance between two sides of the coil on the front side of the armature is called front pitch. (Under the influence of the magnetic field placed in the armature slot in which emf is induced.)

The armature conductor between two coil sides on the front which are connected to the same commutator segment and it is denoted as YF.

The front pitch for both lap and wave winding is odd (1,3,5,7,9……..).

8) Resultant pitch:

It is the distance between two consecutive coils which are connected measured in terms of coil side.It is denoted by YR.

For lap winding,

\[{Y_R} = {Y_B} - {Y_F}\]

For wave winding,

\[{Y_R} = {Y_B} + {Y_F}\]

9) Average pitch:

The average of back pitch and front pitch of a winding is called as average pitch and denoted by YA.

\[{Y_A} = \frac{{{Y_B} + {Y_F}}}{2}\]

Single layer winding:

In this type of winding only one conductor is placed in every slot.

Double layer winding:

In this type of winding two conductors are placed in every slot.

Progressive winding:

If the value of the back pitch is greater than front pitch that winding is said to be a progressive.

\[{Y_B} > {Y_F}\]

Retrogressive winding:

In this type of winding back pitch is less than the front pitch that winding is called retrogressive winding.

\[{Y_B} < {Y_F}\]

Rules for the winding:

  • Both are full pitch that is back pitch and front pitch are each almost equal to pole switch.
  • Both back pitch and front pitch must be odd numbers which helps to design the winding easily.
  • The number of conductor segment is always equal to the number of slots or coils or half the number of conductors.
  • Entire winding must forms a closed path.
  • The average pitch of the lap winding is \(\frac{Z}{P}\) while that for the wave winding is \(\frac{{Z \pm 2}}{P}\).

Applications for windings:

  • The lap winding has the number of path A=P this winding is used for applications which require more current and less voltage. It is possible to obtain higher value of current and less emf will br produce. It requires equalizing connection.
  • This winding is used for the application which requires higher voltage and lesser current since number of parallel path are equal to 2 that is A=2. It gives more emf and equalizing connections are not necessary.

Difference between lap winding and wave winding:

Lap Winding Wave Winding
1. Number of parallel path is equal to poles. (A=P). 1. Number of parallel paths is equal to 2. (A=2).
2. Number of brushes set is requires is equal to the number of poles. 2. Number of brushes set required is always equal to 2.
3. Preferably for high current, low voltage generator. 3. Preferably for low current, high voltage capacity generator.
4. Lap windings are normally used for generators of capacity more than 500A. 4. Wave windings are normally used for generators of capacity less than 500A.
5. Equalizing connections are necessary. 5. Equalizing connections are not necessary.

EMF equation of generator:

Let,

Z = Total number of armature conductors.
P = Number of poles.
N = Speed of armature in RPM.
A = Number of parallel paths in armature. For lap winding A=P, for wave winding A=2.
∅ = Flux produced by each poles in Webbers (wb).
Eg = Generated emf in the armature.

Average emf induced by conductor,

\[e = \frac{{d\emptyset }}{{dt}}\;eqn\;\left( 1 \right)\]

(That is the rate of flux cutting)

In one revolution conductor will cut total flux produced by all the poles is,

\[ = \emptyset \times P\;eqn\;\left( 2 \right)\]

The time required to complete one revolution is,

\[\frac{{60}}{N}\;eqn\;\left( 3 \right)\]

As speed N in RPM that is,

\[e = \frac{{d\emptyset }}{{\frac{{60}}{N}}} = \frac{{\emptyset \times P}}{{\frac{{60}}{N}}}\]

Substitute eqn (1) into (2) and (3)

\[ = \frac{{\emptyset \times P}}{{\frac{{60}}{N}}}\] \[ = \emptyset \times P.\frac{N}{{60}}\] \[ = \frac{{\emptyset PN}}{{60}}\]

In the winding number of conductors with parallel path that is given by \(\frac{Z}{A}\)

Therefore generated emf,

\[{E_g} = \frac{{\emptyset PN}}{{60}} \times \frac{Z}{A}\] \[{E_g} = \frac{{\emptyset PNZ}}{{60A}}\]

For lap winding A=P

\[{E_g} = \frac{{\emptyset PNZ}}{{60P}}\] \[{E_g} = \frac{{\emptyset NZ}}{{60}}\]

For wave winding A=2

\[{E_g} = \frac{{\emptyset PNZ}}{{60 \times 2}}\] \[{E_g} = \frac{{\emptyset PNZ}}{{120}}\]

Types of DC generator:

Depending on the way in which the field winding is excited in the DC generator.

The classification of DC generator is,

  1. Separately excited DC generator
  2. Self-excited DC generator

Separately excited DC generator:

In separately excited generator the field winding is excited from a separate source of DC current.

The field winding is excited separately so field current depends on supply voltage and resistance of the field winding.

In the armature side we can see that the supplying a load, demanding a load current of IL at voltage Vt is called terminal voltage.

\[i.e.,\;{I_a} = {I_L}\]

The power developed in the armature side

\[{P_A} = {E_g}{I_A}\]

Power developed at the load (output)

\[{P_L} = V{I_A}\]

The emf induced E has three component namely,

  1. Terminal voltage (Vt)
  2. Armature resistance drop (IaRa)
  3. Brush contact drop (Vbrush)

So voltage equation for separately excited generator is,

\[E = {V_t} + {I_a}{R_a} + {V_{brush}}\]

Self-excited DC generator:

In this self-excited generator the field winding is excited by the emf developed by the armature itself depending on the way in which field winding is connected to the armature.

The self-excited generators are classified into three types.

  1. Shunt generator
  2. Series generator
  3. Compound generator

Shunt DC generator:

In this type of generator field winding is connected in parallel to the armature winding. More number of turns is connected in shunt field so that the smaller cross section. The resistance of shunt field is more.

Voltage and current relation in the shunt DC generator that is Ia=IL+Ish.

The voltage across the load is Vt is same across field winding. Both are parallel with each other.

\[{I_{sh}} = \frac{{{V_t}}}{{{R_{sh}}}}\]

When emf induced E still requires supplying voltage drop IaRa and brushing contact drop.

Therefore,

\[E = {V_t} + {I_a}{R_a} + {V_{brush}}\]

Where,

\[E = \frac{{N\emptyset ZP}}{{60A}}\]

Series DC generator:

In the series DC generator the field winding is connected in series with armature winding. Series winding consists of few turns so that the larger cross section. The resistance of the series field id very less. Series field winding carries small current of armature.

Voltage and current relationship is,

Field, armature and load are in series hence they carry the same current.

Therefore,

\[{I_A} = {I_{se}} = {I_L}\]

Voltage equation is,

\[E = {V_t} + {I_A}{R_A} + {I_{se}}{R_{se}} + {V_{brush}}\] \[E = {V_t} + {I_A}\left( {{R_A} + {R_{se}}} \right) + {V_{brush}}\]

Compound DC generator:

In this type of generator the field winding is connected in parallel with armature and part in series with armature.

There are two types.

  1. i. Long shunt compound generator
  2. ii. Short shunt compound generator

i) Long shunt compound generator:

In this type shunt field winding is connected across the series combination of armature and series field winding as shown in the diagram.

Voltage and current relationship is,

\[{I_A} = {I_{sh}} + {I_L}\]

And

\[{I_A} = {I_{se}}\]

Voltage across shunt field winding is Vt.

\[{I_{sh}} = \frac{{{V_t}}}{{{R_{sh}}}}\]

Voltage equation is,

\[E = {V_t} + {I_A}{R_A} + {I_A}{R_{se}} + {V_{brush}}\] \[E = {V_t} + {I_A}\left( {{R_A} + {R_{se}}} \right) + {V_{brush}}\]

ii) Short shunt compound generator:

In short shunt compound generator shunt field winding is connected only across the armature excluding series field winding as shown in the diagram.

Voltage and current relationship is,

\[{I_A} = {I_{se}} + {I_{sh}}\]

And

\[{I_{sc}} = {I_L}\]

Therefore,

\[{I_A} = {I_L} + {I_{sh}}\]

Voltage drop across shunt field winding is,

\[E - {I_A}{R_A} - {V_{brush}}\] \[{I_{sh}} = \frac{{E - {I_A}{R_A} - {V_{brush}}}}{{{R_{sh}}}}\]

The voltage equation is,

\[E = {V_t} + {I_A}{R_A} + {I_{se}}{R_{se}} + {V_{brush}}\] \[E = {V_t} + {I_A}{R_A} + {I_L}{R_{se}} + {V_{brush}}\]

Therefore,

\[E - {I_A}{R_A} - {V_{brush}} = {V_t} + {I_L}{R_{sc}}\]

Differential compound generator:

In this generator two windings are wound in such a direction that the fluxes produced by them opposes each other.
