CHAPTER 01 : LOGIC FAMILIES

Logic system:

There are two types in logic system.

  1. Positive logic system
  2. Negetive logic system

Positive logic system: Higher voltage is represented by 1 and lower voltage is represented by 0.

Negative logic system: Higher voltage is represented by 0 and lower voltage is represented by 1.

Example:

Assume we have 5V and 0V as logic level voltage and let us consider +5V as high and 0V as low.

Now, Positive logic system : High = 1 [i.e., 5V] , Low = 0 [i.e., 0V]

Negative logic system : High = 0 [i.e., 5V] , Low = 1 [i.e., 0V]

In practice different notes in digital circuits may differ slightly due to internal resistance and loading effects. Hence a range of voltage undefined logic level.

Definitions

Threshold Voltage:

It is defined as the voltage at the input of a gate which causes a change in the state of output from a logic level to other.

Propagation Delay:

It is the time takes for the output of a gate to change after the inputs have change. The propagation Delay time of a TTL gate is approximately 10 Nano Seconds.

Power Dissipation:

Power dissipation of logic gate is the power required by the gate to operate with 50% duty cycle at a specified frequency. It is expressed as

\[{P_D} = \frac{{{V_{CC}} \times {I_{CC}}}}{n}\]

Where

VCC = Supply Voltage

n = Number of Gates

\({I_C} = \frac{{{I_{CH}} + {I_{CL}}}}{2}\)

PD = Power Dissipation

ICH = Current drawn when output level is high.

ICL = Current drawn when output level is low.

Noise Margin:

It is circuit insensitivity or resistance to Noise. Noise margin is expressed in Volts.

Fan In:

The Fan In of a logic gate is defined as number of input that gate is designed to handle of operate.

Fan Out:

The Fan Out is defined as maximum number of loads that an output can reliably.

Characteristics of TTL:

  • The TTL is the most widely used bipolar digital IC family.
  • The transistor used in TTL logic will be operating in saturated mode.
  • The basic TTL logic circuit is the NAND gate.
  • For standard TTL the input voltage levels are,

    • 0V to 0.8V = Logic 0

    2V to 5V = Logic 1

    0.8V to 2V = Intermediate (Undefined)

Advantages:

  • High speed operation.
  • Low manufacturing cost.
  • Single supply voltage (+5V) with permitted variation of ±0.25V.
  • Commercial and military versions of TTL gates are available.
  • Moderate packing density.

Disadvantages:

  • It has less Fan Out than CMOS.
  • Power Dissipation is much higher than CMOS gate.
  • Cost is higher than CMOS.

Characteristics of ECL:

  • It is also called current mode logic or current steering logic.
  • The transistors are prevented from going into saturation when input changes from low to high.
  • Logic levels are negative and very close to each other.

    • -1.75 = logic 0
    -0.9 = logic 1

  • Voltage swing is limited.

Advantages:

  • ELC circuits produce the output and its complement.
  • Parameters do not vary with temperature.
  • Number of functions available is high.
  • Fan Out is large.

Disadvantages:

  • High cost.
  • It requires negative supply voltage and negative logic level which are not compatible with other logic families.
  • Power consumption is more. Hence power dissipation per gate is large.

Characteristics of CMOS:

  • • It uses complementary (p and n type) MOSFET’s in the same circuit.
  • • CMOS input should never be left disconnected.
  • • Logic levels are indicated as bellow

    • 0V = logic 0
    3V to 15V = logic 1

Advantages:

  • It consumes less power.
  • It can be operated at high voltages.
  • Fan Out is more.
  • Better noise margin.

Disadvantages:

  • Switching speed is low.
  • Greater propagation delay.

IC (Integrated Circuits):

An Integrated Circuit is an electronic circuit formed on a small piece of semiconductor material which performs the same functions as large circuit. It is also called as chip or microchip.

Advantages of IC’s over Discrete Circuits:

  • Size of the IC’s will be small.
  • Cost of the IC's is low.
  • Operating speed is high.
  • IC’s reduces the number of external wiring connections.
  • Reliability is more when compared to discrete circuits.
  • Reduced power consumption makes the digital system more economical to operate.
