CHAPTER 04 : ENERGY CONSERVATION

Energy conservation in a residential building:

  • Turn off lights when not in use.
  • Take advantage of sunlight and natural air.
  • Clean the lamp to maintain illumination.
  • Use fluorescent lamp, CFL lamp, LED lamp rather than incandescent lamp.
  • Use solar water heater instead of electric water heater.
  • Do not keep TV, Music player and any other in standby mode.
  • Do not open the doors of refrigerator frequently.
  • Use moderate flame settings to conserve LPG.
  • Prevent illuminating the entire room.
  • Use stabilizers with electrical appliances.
  • Avoid use of decorative lighting.
  • Select iron boxes with automatic temperature cutoff.
  • Avoid dry grinding in your food mixer as it takes longer time then liquid grinding.

Energy Conservation in Commercial Sectors:

  • Construct building so that maximum use of sunlight is utilized.
  • Avoid use of decorative lighting.
  • Turn off all the lights other than security lights when not in use.
  • Use AC if necessary.
  • Turn off your office equipment’s when not in use.
  • Use wiring of proper size to reduce energy loss.
  • Operate Lift during busy hours only.
  • Use energy efficient lamps.
  • Use proper selective glass so that the solar rays easily enter in the building.

Energy Conservation in Industrial Sector:

  • By using more efficient motors and avoiding over size motors for power needed.
  • By improving power factor by employing shunt capacitors across motor terminals.
  • Use thyristor control DC motors to get variable speed.
  • Compulsory energy audit is necessary for industrial units.
  • Use mercury vapor lamp instead of incandescent lamp for better illumination.
  • Installing accurate load monitoring and recording system.
  • Employing processes that reduce specific energy consumption while making steel.
  • Avoid under size cables for connections.
  • Regularly checking and monitoring the units.
  • Improving mechanical efficiency of the drives to which the motors are coupled.
  • Selecting voltage taps in a induction melting furnace.

Energy Conservation in Agricultural Sector:

  • Using pumps and motors of high efficiency.
  • Avoiding unnecessary running of motors.
  • Adapting starters with motors.
  • Replacing conventional pump sets by mano black pump sets.
  • Use ISO marked pumps and motors.
  • Proper alignment of pump and motor.
  • Using a larger pipe saves 15% of energy.
  • Use of low resistance foot valve save 10% of energy.
  • Use suction pipes of rigid PVC pipes.
  • Keep delivery pipe clos to ground level as possible.
  • Replace the damage part.
  • Avoid loos contacts and check the terminals.

Energy Conservation in Transmission and Distribution Lines:

In case of transmission and distribution lines there are two types of losses.

  1. Technical losses: The technical losses are due to inadequate sub transmission and distribution lines, improper sizing of conductors used, lengthy transmission and distribution lines and inadequate reactive compensation in the system. The technical losses are also classified as variable loss and fixed loss.
  2. Non-technical losses: Non-technical losses are also known as commercial losses. These losses are due to the theft of energy, meters not read, defective, inaccurate, non-recording meters, bills not prepared, inaccurate billing and no disconnection of service for non-payment.

Measuring of Optimize Transmission Distribution Lines:

The measuring of TD lines are classified into two types.

1. Technical Measures:

  • Stepping up transformation at the generating station.
  • Extra high voltage transmission system.
  • Step-down transformation at substation levels.
  • Use of low resistance conductors such as aluminum.
  • Selecting appropriate capacity of distribution transformer on maximum demand.
  • Encouraging domestic and commercial consumer to use only energy efficient lamps and solar water heater for hot water requirement.
  • A conductor size should be selected so that will carry the required load with specified voltage drop limits.
  • Applying shunt capacitors across individual inductive loads for power factor improvements.

2. Non-technical Measures:

  • Optimal use of distribution transformer based on their load factor to enhance their operational efficiency.
  • Limiting peak loads on HV and EHV transmission lines.

Energy Efficient Devices:

Energy efficiency is the good effort to reduce the amount of energy required for services. It is also defined as using less energy to provide the same service. For example a fluorescent bulb uses less energy compare to incandescent bulb to produce same amount of light.

Terms Related to Energy Efficiency:

Various Motivations to Improve Energy Efficiency:

Power Quality:

Introduction: Power quality is the set of limits of electrical properties that allows electrical system to function in their intendant manner without significant loss of performance or like the term is use to describe electric power that drive all electrical load and the load ability to function property if that electrical power without the proper power and electrical load not operate. These are many ways in which electric power can be poor quality and many more causes of such poor quality power.

Definition: Poor quality is a set of electrical boundary that allows a equipment to function in a correct manner without loss of performance.

Power Quality Parameters:

  • Variation in voltage and current.
  • Continuity in service.
  • Transient voltages and current.
  • Harmonics content in the wave form.
  • Current and voltage unbalance.
  • Flicker.
  • Voltage dips and short term over voltage.
  • Phase shifting and reactive power.

Common Power Quality Problem:

  • Very Short Interruption: Total interruption of electrical supply for duration from few mile seconds to 1 or 2 seconds.
  • Long Interruption: Total interruption of electrical supply for duration greater than 1 or 2 seconds.
  • Voltage Spike: Very fast variation if the voltage value for duration from mile seconds to few mile seconds.
  • Voltage Fluctuations: The variation of input voltage or amplitude for certain time or oscillation of voltage value.
  • Voltage Unbalance: The voltage variation in 3 phase system in which the 3 voltage magnitudes or phase angle difference between them are not equal.
  • Noise: Super imposing of high frequency signals on the wave form of the power system.
  • Harmonic Distortion: Voltage and current wave form assume non-sinusoidal shape. It means Sin waves with different magnitude and phase.
  • Voltage Sag or Dip: Due to this normal voltage level decrease between 10 to 90% of RMS value.

Remedies for Power Quality Problems:

  • Good Earthing Practice: Large number of power quality problems are caused by incorrect earthing practices. Hence proper earthing practice should be done.
  • Use of UPS: When there is main disturbance in the main supply in which reduction in the main voltage bellow the normal level the UPS will help to avoid the causes. Therefore standby UPS, online UPS and hybrid Ups are used.
  • Active Filters: These are used to maintain the proper power factor. This is achieved by using inductors and capacitors in parallel with the load in the power system.
  • Passive Filter: These are connected to equipment’s to remove high order harmonic frequencies from the supply.
  • Static Breaker: It is used for isolation of faulted circuits in the shortest possible time which improves the power quality.

Power Factor Impedance:

Introduction: PF is measurement of how efficiently a facility uses electrical energy. A hoig PF means electrical capacity is utilized effectively, where low PF indicates poor utilization of electrical power. PF involves the relationship between these 2 types of power. Active power measured in kilo watts (kW) and reactive power measured in kilo volt ampere (KVAR). Active and reactive power makes apparent power which is measured in KVA.

Definition: PF is a ratio between active power and apparent power. Active power does work and reactive power produces an electromagnetic field for inductive loads.

Correcting Poor Power Factor:

Adding capacitance can correct poor PF. PF correction capacitors supplies the necessary reactive portion of power for inductive devices. So this generally results in a reduction in total consumer demand and energy charges. A PF correction requirement determines the total amount of capacitors required at the low voltage levels. PF improvement devices such as capacitors should be installed near the load for improvement.

Power Factor Improvements Methods:

The following equipment’s are methods are used for PF improvements.

  1. Static Capacitor
  2. Synchronous condenser
  3. Phase advancer
  1. Static Capacitor:

    2. These are connected in parallel with the industrial load which works on low PF. Capacitor provides leading current which neutralize lagging inductive components and thus PF is improved which results in energy conservation and improve efficiency of the system. A low PF is expensive and inefficient and sometimes companies may charge additional electricity fees when the PF is less than 0.9.

  2. Synchronous Condenser:

    3. Over excited synchronous motor is called as synchronous condenser, when it is connected across the supply voltage then it draws leading current and partially eliminates the reactive components and hence PF is improved and results in energy conservation.

  3. Phase Advancers:

    4. There are simply AC exciter which is connected on the main shaft of the motor and operates with the motor rotor circuits for PF improvement. In industry for induction motor these are used. Whenever PF of motor is improved it leads to energy conservation.

Energy Conservation Act 2001:

Energy conservation act 2001 was enacted on 1st October 2001 and became effective from 1st March 2002. It was enacted by the govt. of India. The purpose or objectives of the act is to provide,

  1. For efficient use of energy and its conservation.
  2. Provide a policy for energy conservation activities.
  3. Co-ordinate policies and programs on efficient use of energy with holders.
  4. Establish system and procedure to verify measures and monitor energy improvements.
  5. To promote energy efficiency in the country.

Features of Energy Conservation Act:

  1. Energy Conservation Building Code (ECBC).
  2. Standards and Labeling (S&L).
  3. Demand Side Management (DSM).
  4. Bachat Lamp Yojana (BLY).
  5. Promoting energy efficiency in Small and Medium Industry (SMES).
  6. Prescribe energy consumers.
  7. Certification of energy managers and energy auditors.
