CHAPTER 02 : MAINTENANCE AND TOTAL QUALITY MANAGEMENT

Definition

The work of keeping something in proper condition is called maintenance. The main purpose of regular maintenance is to check that all the equipment required for protection is operating at 100% efficiency at all times. So daily inspection, cleaning, lubricating and making minor adjustment, minor problems can be detected and corrected before they become the major problem that can shut down the whole production product.

Types of Maintenance:

  1. Breakdown maintenance: In this type of maintenance no care is taken for the machine until the equipment fail. Then repair is under taken later. This type of maintenance is used when the equipment failure does not affect the operation or production process and loss due to equipment failure, hence breakdown maintenance is avoided.

  2. Preventive maintenance: It is also called as plan maintenance. It is important function to keep the equipment in good operation condition. It includes daily maintenance such as cleaning, inspection, oiling and retighten. To retain the healthy condition of equipment and prevent failure the minor problems can be detected and get repair avoid the equipment completely fails. If equipment fails the whole production process will be affected hence to avoid such problems preventive maintenance is necessary.

Objectives of Preventive Maintenance:

  • To obtain maximum availability of the plant by avoiding breakdown and by reducing the shut down time to minimum.
  • To keep the machine in proper condition to maintain the quality of the product.
  • To ensure safety of the workers.
  • To keep the plant at maximum production efficiency.
  • To reduce the economic cost due to shut down process.

Advantages of Preventive Maintenance:

  • Reduction in production time.
  • Less number of stand by equipment, less spare parts are needed.
  • Less expenditure on repairs.
  • Greater safety to the operator.
  • Increases the equipment life.
  • Energy saving.
  • Reduction in process shut down.

Quality:

It is the ability of the product which satisfies the end user or customer.

Quality control:

It is nothing but controlling the quality of the product during manufacturing is called quality control.

Inspection:

Inspection is determining the function from raw materials supply, parts, spare parts and other accessories acceptable or not.

Factors affecting Quality:

  • Money.
  • Men, machines, materials and manufacturing conditions.
  • Management policies and inadequate space.
  • Poor packing.
  • Inappropriate transportation.
  • Production methods and product design.
  • Poor sales and service.

Objectives of Quality Control:

  • Proper utilization of material, machines and men for maintaining quality of product.
  • Proper control of wastage of raw materials.
  • Prevention of defect in the product.
  • Proper attention should be given of the time of the packing and transportation of the product.
  • Preparation of quality control reports and their submission to the management.
  • To make different measures to improve the quality standards of the product.
  • To have quality audit of various departments in the factory. Conducting the quality in the work places.

Functions of Quality Control:

  • Only the standard quality material should be purchased before manufacturing the product.
  • To maintain the quality value at each stage.
  • To prevent the damage product are defective products reaching to customer.
  • To provide information regarding improvement on production process.

Advantages of Quality Control:

  • It helps in maintaining the quality of a product.
  • It rejection rate will decreases.
  • Inspection cost is reduced.
  • There is improved relationship between producer and customer.
  • The sales of the product will improve hence more relation and profit to the company.
  • Due to standard production methods, production cost will decrease.

Inspection:

It is the function to judge the quality of the product. In can be also be defined as the act as comparing the materials, products according it performance with the standards.

Inspection is nothing but judge the product is of right quality are not. It is essential to reduce wastage by avoiding the defects in the manufacturing stage.

Functions of Inspection Department:

  • To inspect raw materials which are required for production.
  • To inspect the material properties of material like strength, hardness, roughness, stiffness and ductility.
  • To inspect all small items like nut, bolt, screws. Which are directly purchased from outside.
  • To inspect the product in all the production stages.
  • To collect the data from the customer regarding the performance of the product.
  • To prevent the defective item reaching to the customer.

Tools and Techniques of Total Quality Management:

  • Flow chart
  • Pareto chart
  • Cause and effect diagram
  • 5S method
  • Pokayoke
  • Kaizen
  • Six sigma

Flow Chart:

Flow chart is schematic diagram of the sequence of steps involved in a process. It provides a simple way to use and understand. By seeing the steps involved in a process, every one develops a clear picture of how the operation works and where problems could arrives?

Pareto Chart:

It is a technic used to identify quality problems based on their importance. The logic behind this char is only a few quality problems are important where as many other are not critical. This concept has been also called as 80/20 Rule. It also nothing but the most quality problems is only due to few causes. This chart is used to identify these problems.

Cause and Effect Diagram:

This cause identify particular quality problem. It is also called as fish bone diagram because they look like bones of fish. The head of the fish is the quality problem. The causes related to machines, workers, measurements, suppliers, materials and many others.

For example, A problem with environment and the workplace could be due to improper ventilation, lighting, dust and temperature. The cause and effect diagram is problem solving tool commonly used by quality control team.

5S Methodology:

5S means Sorting, straightening, sweeping, standard and sustaining.

In Japanese language Sorting-Seiri, straightening-Seiton, Sweeping-Seiso, Standard-Seiketsu, Sustaing-Shitsuke.

Sorting: Eliminate all unnecessary tools, parts and instructions. Go through all the work area. Keep only essential items and eliminate what is not required.

Straightening or setting in order: There should be a place for everything. The place for each item should be clearly labeled. Item should be arranged in proper manor so that it should be available efficiently in the workplace. Workers should not have to bend repeatedly to access materials. Each tool, equipment should be kept close to the workplace.

Sweeping: Keep the workplace neat and clean. At the end of each shift clean the work area. This makes it easy to know what goes where and where it belongs. Maintaining cleanness should be part of daily work.

Standard: All work stations for a particular job should be identical. All employees doing the same job should be able to work in any station with the same tools that are in the same location in every station.

Sustaining or self-discipline: Once the previous 4S have been established they become an important tool to follow the steps when an issue arises make changes as appropriate.

Pokayoke:

Pokayoke is a Japanese word that means error prevention or mistake proofing. A pokayoke is a mechanism in manufacturing process that helps an operator to avoid (yoke) mistakes (poka). Its purpose is to eliminate product defects by human errors. The aim of pokayoke is to design the process so that mistakes can be detected and corrected immediately by eliminating defects at the time.

Anybody should practice pokayoke in the workplace. It is usable by all workers, simple to install, low cost.

Pokayoke can be used wherever something can go wrong or an error can be made. It is a technic that can be applied to any type of process in manufacturing or service industry where human errors can occur.

The types of errors are,

  1. Processing error
  2. Setup error
  3. Missing part
  4. Improper part
  5. Operation error
  6. Measurement errors

Benefits of Pokayoke:

  • Less time spend on training workers.
  • Eliminations of many operations related to quality control.
  • Promotion of the work improvement.
  • Reduced number of rejects.
  • Immediate action when a problem occurs.
  • 100% quality control.

Kaizen:

Kai means ‘Change’, Zen means ‘Good’.

Kaizen is the practice of continuous improvement. Today kaizen is recognized worldwide as an important pillar of an organization. It is used on certain principles.

  1. Good process brings good qualities or results.
  2. Take action to correct causes of problems.
  3. Work as a team.

Kaizen means everyone involved in making improvements. The kaizen is the daily process the purpose is simple productivity improvements. It is also a process when correctly done, eliminates over hard work and teaches people how to perform experiments on their work and how to learn to spot and eliminate waste in business process.

In short Kaizen means change for the better by everybody everyday everywhere.

Six Sigma:

It is a set of practice designed to improving manufacturing process and eliminate defects. In six sigma a defect is defined as any process output that does not meet specification. The adapting six sigma a continuous effort to achieve process results for business. By 6 sigma method the manufacturing process can be measured, analyze, improved and controlled. A special quality improvement process is adapted through six sigma method. A special infra-structure champion, master black belt, only black belt, green belt etc. to lead and implement the six sigma approach.

Quality Management System:

ISO 9000 family of certificate of help the organization to fulfill the customer requirements by helping the organization in improving the quality.

The standards are applicable all type of company have global acceptance in many industries ISO certification as become the requirement for doing business.

The following 3 new standards are,

  1. ISO 9000:2000: It is the starting point for understanding the system of standards. It provides definition used in the standards.
  2. ISO 9001:2000: This is the standard use for customer requirements. It is used to demonstrate the quality system to need the customer requirement.
  3. ISO 9004:2000: It provides guidelines for establishing the quality management system. It produces not only one meeting customer requirements but also an improving performance.

ISO 9000 Certification:

  • Training should be given to all the employees who are in the organization in order to bring awareness.
  • To create a task process or team.
  • Analyze the existing particles and take correct action.
  • Design and develop standards procedure.
  • Prepare quality manual, quality procedure and quality work instructions.
  • Implement the quality system.
  • Quality avoiding and preliminary quality audit outside party.
  • Apply for certification.
  • Maintain the system.

ISO Audit:

It is an assignment carried out to establish the organization to meet the requirements according to standards. Audit helps in detecting the short form (defects) in the procedure and process. It is necessary for issuing ISO certification. It is classified as internal and external audit.

  • Internal Audit: Audit done by internal team belonging to the organization is called internal audit. It helps to spread the awareness among the organization members. The drawbacks pointed out during internal audit can be corrected before an external audit.
  • External Audit: Audit done by external independent agency is called external audit. It is necessary for ISO certification. External audit is capable of issuing an ISO certification for the organization. It satisfies all the requirements of ISO quality standards.

Identification of Nonconformance:

It is an outcome of audit either internal or external. Those activities which do not satisfy the quality problem directly or indirectly, and which does not satisfy the customer requirements such activities are called nonconformance. The nonconformance may be a minor or a major depending on it affects the quality system of the company. Too many nonconformance decreases the quality system of the organization. Therefore steps should be taken to reduce number of nonconformance.

Preventive action is necessary to avoid any failure of quality system. When nonconformance is detected, corrected action has to be taken to remove them. This maintenance of quality system within the organization improves the effectiveness of quality system.

Benefits of ISO Certification:

  • It provides reputation in domestic and global market.
  • It provides a climate for consistence improvement in the quality of product.
  • It reduces wastage.
  • It changes the attitude of work and work atmosphere.
  • The product of right quality, customer friendly is reduced.
  • Number of customer is increased.
